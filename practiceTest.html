I have stranded in an Island and this is my servival kit page. Most people have a really hard time being left alone with their own mind, which is full of destruction and misery. People spend their time distracting themselves. I stranded in island to know myself and to learn from earth, plants and animals what i need to know to move on in life.

Big Pot

Surviving on a deserted island includes finding fresh water.
If you do not have water within a few days, you will definitely croak. Once you find a fresh water supply, you will want a large pot to boil it in. This will also be handy when cooking fish and any other meats you acquire.


Sleeping bag

Sleeping under the stars wrapped up in a green sleeping bad sounds enticing, considering you'll be protected from the bugs. Protecting yourself from anything that wants to crawl on you when you're sleeping is a must. A sleeping bag may be an underrated object to bring on an island, but is essential.

Lighter

Fire is going to be absolutely essential to your island experience. Not only will it cook your fish and wild boar, but it will be your nighttime lamp and consistent morale booster. I don’t know about you, but starting a fire with two sticks is not something I’m going to magically learn how to do. Bring some lighters and sleep happily by the crackling light of a small fire that will warm you if the air turns cold and scare away predators.


Knife

What’s the big number one that we would take with us to a desert island? A knife of course!
You can use it to open coconuts, make spears for fishing, and crack crab legs. And if the knife’s big enough (think machete), you can even use it to cut wood for your shelter and camp fires.

Cocos Island

Where is Cocos Island?
The Cocos Islands are in a remote region of the Pacific, some 340 miles off the western coast of Costa Rica.

What can you find in Cocos Island?
Covered by vast, tropical jungle, the island is best known as an outstanding diving destination, luring divers with its remote beauty and seas teeming with life

My Plan of survival in Cocos Island?
STOP and Think . Use the Boy Scouts mnemonic device of "STOP", which stands for "Stop, Think, Observe, and Plan". You've just discovered that you are alone on an island. Here is the list, in order, of what you should be doing in order of priority:
  • Find a source of drinking water.
  • Find/build a shelter.
  • Build a fire.
  • Find a source of food.
  • Create tools for catching food.
  • Fashion weapons for self defense.
  • Create a raft to leave the island.
